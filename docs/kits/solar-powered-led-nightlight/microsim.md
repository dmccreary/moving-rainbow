# MicroSim of Solar Powered Nightlight

This interactive simulation lets you explore the solar nightlight circuit
without building it first. Move the sun across the sky and watch the
battery charge during the day and the lamp glow at night.

!!! tip "Pixel says..."
    Try dragging the sun down below the horizon. See how the lamp wakes
    up when it gets dark? That's the transistor doing its job!

## Open the Simulation

These versions run in the p5.js editor. Click a link to open and run it:

- [Version 3 (newest)](https://editor.p5js.org/dmccreary/sketches/9sQPAz6sf)
- [Version 2](https://editor.p5js.org/dmccreary/sketches/5rKRUamW-)

## What to Look For

- **Daytime:** The solar panel feeds current through the diode and the
  battery charge level rises. The lamp stays off.
- **Nighttime:** The panel voltage drops, the transistor switches on, and
  the lamp lights up using stored battery power.
